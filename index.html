Yujin-Julian Litz Park

I am currently a highschool senior student at Point Grey Secondary. I am fluent in three languages (in order of proficiency): English, German, and Korean. Computer Science is my passion, and I have competed in various math, programming, and cybersecurity(CTF) competitions since grade 8.

CTF Competitions

CTF(Capture the Flag) contests are cybersecurity competitions in which participants solve computer security problems and/or capture and defend computer systems. I first discovered CTF competitions in grade 8, where I self-studied programming languages and various techniques in order to compete. Even challenges that I was unable to solve taught me a lot from my failed attempts. Recently, I have learning assembly in order to better solve reverse-engineering and binary-exploitation challenges.

Games

Especially during the covid-19 pandemic, games have been a way to relax and have fun with my friends. I enjoy playing a variety of genres, some of my favorites being CS:GO, Tabletop Simulator, Among Us, and Party Animals. In addition, I have met a large number of people, all from different backgrounds and walks of life. Although it can be easy to lose track of time when having fun, I make sure to time my playing-sessions in order to stay productive.

Programming and Automation

I was first introduced to computer science at Norma Rose Point while I was in grade 6. We were given the opportunity to design our own (basic) website. Ever since I have been interested in developing various programs and websites. While creating various projects, such as an automatic file decryptor (for a ctf challenge), and a word descrambler, I learned how to use Python, Java, and C. In addition, affter reading the book Automate the Boring Stuff with Python, by Al Sweigart, automating various small and time consuming tasks has made my life a lot easier.
